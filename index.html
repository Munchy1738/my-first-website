The Human Body

Your body is an incredible machine! Here are some fascinating facts:

  • Your brain contains about 86 billion neurons (nerve cells)
  • Your heart beats about 100,000 times per day
  • You have about 37 trillion cells in your body
  • Your bones are stronger than steel, but much lighter
  • You blink about 15-20 times per minute
Did you know? Your body replaces all of its cells every 7-10 years. That means you're literally a different person than you were a decade ago!

Water Cycle

Water on Earth constantly moves through a cycle:

  • Evaporation: Water from oceans, lakes, and rivers turns into vapor
  • Condensation: Water vapor cools and forms clouds
  • Precipitation: Water falls as rain, snow, or hail
  • Collection: Water collects in bodies of water and the cycle starts again

States of Matter

Solid

Particles are tightly packed together. Example: ice, rocks, wood

Liquid

Particles flow freely. Example: water, juice, oil

Gas

Particles spread out to fill space. Example: air, steam, oxygen

Plasma

Super hot ionized gas. Example: lightning, stars, neon signs

Fractions Basics

A fraction has two parts:

  • Numerator: The top number (how many parts you have)
  • Denominator: The bottom number (total number of parts)

Example: In 3/4, you have 3 parts out of 4 total parts.

Remember: When adding or subtracting fractions, you need the same denominator (bottom number)!

The Solar System

Our solar system has 8 planets that orbit around the Sun. Here they are in order from closest to farthest:

  • Mercury: Smallest planet, very hot during the day
  • Venus: Hottest planet due to its thick atmosphere
  • Earth: Our home! The only planet with life we know of
  • Mars: The "Red Planet" with the largest volcano in the solar system
  • Jupiter: Largest planet, with a giant storm called the Great Red Spot
  • Saturn: Famous for its beautiful rings made of ice and rock
  • Uranus: Tilted on its side, appears blue-green
  • Neptune: Farthest planet, has the strongest winds
Amazing Fact: Light from the Sun takes 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth!

The Moon

Earth's Moon is our closest neighbor in space:

  • It takes 27.3 days to orbit Earth
  • The same side always faces Earth
  • Neil Armstrong was the first person to walk on the Moon in 1969
  • The Moon causes ocean tides on Earth
  • It has no atmosphere, so there's no weather

What is Programming?

Programming is like giving instructions to a computer. Just like you follow a recipe to bake a cake, computers follow code to do tasks!

Popular Programming Languages

Python

Great for beginners! Used for websites, games, and AI.

JavaScript

Makes websites interactive (like this one!)

Scratch

Perfect for kids! Drag and drop blocks to code.

HTML/CSS

Creates the structure and style of websites.

How Computers Work

  • Input: You give information (keyboard, mouse)
  • Processing: The CPU (brain) processes the data
  • Storage: Information is saved (hard drive, memory)
  • Output: Results are shown (screen, speakers)
Want to start coding? Try websites like Scratch.mit.edu or Code.org - they're free and perfect for beginners!

Internet Safety Tips

  • Never share personal information online (address, phone number)
  • Use strong passwords with letters, numbers, and symbols
  • Always tell a trusted adult if something online makes you uncomfortable
  • Think before you post - things online can last forever
  • Be kind online, just like you are in person

Ancient Civilizations

Ancient Egypt

Built pyramids, invented paper from papyrus, lasted over 3,000 years!

Ancient Greece

Invented democracy, Olympics, and made advances in math and science.

Ancient Rome

Built roads and aqueducts, created a vast empire, Latin language.

Ancient China

Invented paper, gunpowder, compass, and the printing press.

Important Inventions

  • Wheel (3500 BC): Changed transportation forever
  • Printing Press (1440): Made books available to everyone
  • Light Bulb (1879): Thomas Edison brought light to homes
  • Telephone (1876): Alexander Graham Bell connected the world
  • Internet (1960s-1990s): Connected computers globally
  • Smartphone (2007): A computer in your pocket!
Did you know? The Great Wall of China is over 13,000 miles long and took over 2,000 years to build!

Seven Continents

  • Asia: Largest continent, 4.6 billion people
  • Africa: Second largest, birthplace of humanity
  • North America: Includes Canada, USA, and Mexico
  • South America: Home to Amazon rainforest
  • Europe: Many different countries and cultures
  • Australia: Smallest continent, unique wildlife
  • Antarctica: Coldest, covered in ice, no permanent residents
